Protect The Protest

Throughout history, protest has been a powerful tool for change. But governments around the world are cracking down on protests and it must be protected.

Governments around the world are restricting people’s right to protest, because too many people in power fear change. Too many want to maintain the status quo. Too many want to keep people divided.

By coming together and ensuring that everyone – especially the marginalised and discriminated against – can participate in protests, we can create a more just and equal world. Together, we must protect the protest wherever it is restricted, whenever it is at risk. We must come together to create a world where people can peacefully demand change without persecution.

Stand With Detained Iranian Students

A Revolutionary Court in Iran has handed down a sentence of 16 years in prison to university students, Ali Younesi & Amir Houssein Moradi, following a unjust trial.

During court proceedings, both students recounted enduring numerous instances of torture and ill-treatment such as a 60-day period of solitary confinement.

They are both prisoners of conscience who have been singled out by the oppressive regime in Iran, a fate shared by many other students who have taken a stand against the dictatorship.
